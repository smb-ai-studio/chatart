SEEDANCE 2 MODEL GUIDE

Seedance 2 guide for multimodal AI video assets.

Seedance 2 is best understood as a reference-led video model route: it is useful when a campaign video needs visual references, motion direction, audio-aware pacing, and more deliberate control than a plain text-to-video prompt.

CAPABILITIES

Capabilities to understand before you prompt.

Multimodal references

Use text, image, video, and audio direction to explain style, subject, motion, and pace more clearly than text alone.

Director-style control

Write prompts like a shot list: subject, action, lighting, shadow, camera movement, sound mood, and final frame.

Motion stability

Useful for product reveal clips, creator scenes, and campaign ideas where movement should feel intentional rather than random.

Audio-video planning

Helpful when the video needs rhythm, ambience, music direction, or a mood that supports social and ad creative.

PROMPT STARTER

A practical prompt to adapt in ChatArt Pro.

Copy-paste prompt

Create a cinematic 4K product reveal video for [product]. Use the uploaded product image as the main reference. Slow dolly-in, soft rim light, realistic reflections, premium studio background, subtle audio rhythm, final frame holds with clean space for headline and CTA.

HOW TO USE IT

Turn the model into a practical prompt workflow.

  1. Start with the job. Define the exact asset you need: ad concept, launch video, product image, social post, caption set, or campaign brief.
  2. Add production constraints. Include audience, channel, format, brand tone, visual references, and what must stay consistent.
  3. Generate a proof. Review the first output for clarity, consistency, text readability, motion, or factual accuracy before scaling.
  4. Build a practice set. Use the strongest direction to practice supporting visuals, captions, scripts, music direction, and creative variants in ChatArt Pro.

PRODUCTION RECIPE

Reference-led video production recipe.

Use Seedance 2 when the brief already has a visual reference, product image, or mood direction. Start by creating one hero clip, then ask ChatArt Pro to derive the supporting thumbnail, caption hooks, music mood, and paid-social variant from the same scene. Review product continuity, camera movement, final-frame CTA space, and whether the clip still communicates without sound.
