CAPABILITIES

Where Nano Banana shines.

Rapid ideation

Use it to generate multiple directions from the same brief: different moods, color palettes, settings, compositions, and art styles.

Expressive visual range

Good for moodboards, thumbnails, visual metaphors, character directions, and playful brand exploration before the final style is decided.

Reference-based exploration

Use an uploaded image or a precise description as a starting point, then ask for alternate lighting, background, tone, or format variations.

Bridge to bigger assets

WHEN TO USE IT

Choose Nano Banana when you still want options.

Early-stage brainstorming

Use it before the brief is locked, when variety is more valuable than final polish.

Mood and style boards

Generate visual territories that help a team decide whether the work should feel premium, playful, surreal, editorial, or cinematic.

PROMPT STARTERS

Ask for range, not just one answer.

Style spread

Create five visual directions for [idea]: minimalist, cinematic, playful, luxury, and surreal. Keep the core subject recognizable.

Moodboard frame

A moodboard image for [campaign], combining [colors], [texture], [setting], and [emotion], suitable as a creative direction reference.

Variant pass

Keep the same subject, but create alternate backgrounds and lighting: studio, outdoor golden hour, neon night, and clean editorial.

LIMITS TO CHECK

Move to a polish pass when the direction is chosen.

Nano Banana is most useful for range and discovery. For final branded assets, inspect details carefully and consider moving the selected direction into GPT Image 2 or a design workflow for tighter control.
