CONTENT POLICY

Clear, reviewable product information for creators and marketers.

This public knowledge base explains ChatArt Pro asset production workflows, model planning notes, and comparison guidance while pointing readers to the app as the current product source of record.

How to use these pages

  • Use the public pages for planning workflows, comparing creative routes, and understanding how ChatArt Pro can help turn a brief into campaign-ready assets.
  • Use the linked model guides to plan prompts, references, review criteria, and next-step assets before opening the app.
  • Use the ChatArt Pro app to confirm current availability, output options, pricing, regions, commercial-use terms, and product experience.
  • Pages do not imply access to models, plans, output rights, or features not explicitly available in the current product workflow.

REVIEW STANDARDS

What every public page should do.

The site is maintained as a practical knowledge base, not a generic model directory.

Answer a real production task

Each page should help a creator or marketer decide, create, compare, review, or ship an asset pack.

Separate guidance from availability

Public model descriptions help with planning. Current model access, terms, and export options must be confirmed in the app.

Show practical next steps

Guides should include workflows, prompt starters, decision tables, checklists, or concrete use cases where possible.

Keep claims reviewable

Freshness-sensitive statements should include a review date, source note, or a clear reminder to confirm the current product experience.

MAINTENANCE WORKFLOW

How pages should be updated.

  1. 01

    Check the reader task

    Confirm whether the page helps with a creator workflow, marketing campaign, model choice, prompt structure, or product planning question.

  2. 02

    Review source-sensitive claims

    For models, features, pricing, access, and terms, use current reliable sources and avoid overstating certainty.

  3. 03

    Improve practical value

    Add examples, checklists, prompt starters, asset maps, or decision guidance when a page feels thin.

  4. 04

    Verify discovery basics

    Confirm internal links, canonical URL, sitemap entry, llms.txt reference, structured data, GA4, GSC, and mobile usability.

WHAT WE AVOID

No unsupported hype, hidden assumptions, or thin summaries.

No fake certainty

If model controls, regions, export settings, or commercial-use terms may change, the page should say to confirm the current app experience.

No generic filler

Pages should add ChatArt Pro-specific workflow value instead of repeating broad AI model descriptions.

No doorway pages

Pages should be useful on their own and should not exist only to target a phrase or redirect a reader elsewhere.

No broken production trail

Important public pages should link naturally to related model guides, comparisons, articles, FAQ, or the app CTA.
