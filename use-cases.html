  2. 02

  3. 03

  4. 04

    Package channel variants

    Adapt the asset set for social posts, ad tests, product launches, creator content, and campaign handoff.

USE CASE RECIPES

What to create for each common marketing job.

Start from the business moment, then build the minimum useful asset pack before expanding into more variants.

New product launch

Create one hero video, one product key visual, three thumbnail directions, five launch captions, and three paid-social hook variants. Use this when the team needs a first campaign kit fast.

Weekly creator post

Create one short video idea, one cover image, one caption set, one music mood, and two alternate hooks. Use this when a creator has a topic but not a finished post package.

Ad creative refresh

Create three visual routes, three opening hooks, three headline options, and one landing-page angle for each route. Use this when performance is declining and the team needs new concepts.

Campaign concept review

Create a moodboard, hero scene, product shot, script outline, and message hierarchy. Use this before stakeholder review so the team can judge a direction, not just a paragraph of copy.

STARTING PROMPT

Bring one rough brief and ask for a complete asset pack.

Campaign asset prompt

Turn this brief into a campaign asset pack for [audience] on [channel]. Product: [product]. Offer: [offer]. Desired emotion: [emotion]. Create: one video concept, one key visual direction, one thumbnail idea, five caption hooks, three ad headlines, and a music mood. Keep the tone [brand tone] and leave space for final CTA text.

After the first generation, choose the strongest direction and ask ChatArt Pro to expand it into channel-specific variants instead of starting from scratch again.

DELIVERY CHECK

A use case is ready when the assets can be reviewed together.

Message consistency

The video, image, caption, and ad copy all point to the same audience, offer, and emotional angle.

Channel fit

The aspect ratio, opening hook, visual density, and caption length match the platform where the asset will be used.

CTA space

The final frame or key visual leaves enough room for headline, button copy, logo, or product message.

Rights and review

Before paid or client delivery, confirm product terms, model availability, commercial-use rights, and brand/legal requirements in the current workflow.
